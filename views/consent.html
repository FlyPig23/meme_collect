Research Study Consent Form

We invite you to participate in a research study being conducted by investigators from Texas A&M University. You are being asked to participate in this research study because you can contribute to our understanding of cross-cultural meme interpretation.

Purpose of the Study

The aim of this research is to explore how memes are interpreted differently across cultures and to build a dataset that can help understand cultural misunderstandings in online communication.

Study Procedures

If you agree to participate, you will:

  • Complete a brief demographic survey
  • Upload at least 10 memes that you're familiar with
  • Provide detailed "Cultural Context" explanations for each meme, and "Potential Misunderstandings"
  • Label each meme with:
    • A sentiment label (choose one: positive, negative, or neutral)
    • Emotion labels (can select multiple: sarcastic, humorous, motivation, offensive)

Data Usage

We would like to use the data we obtain in this study for current research as well as future studies. These studies may provide additional information that will be helpful in understanding cross-cultural communication and developing better tools for cultural exchange online.

Confidentiality

Your data will be stored without any personally identifiable information. The memes and explanations you provide will be used for research purposes only and will be stored securely.

Risks and Benefits

There are no known risks from being in this study. While you will not benefit personally, your contributions will help improve cross-cultural understanding in online spaces.

Contact Information

If you have any questions about the research study, please contact Professor Meng Xia (mengxia@tamu.edu) or Hangxiao Zhu (hangxiao@tamu.edu).

If you do not wish to participate, you may simply close this browser tab.
